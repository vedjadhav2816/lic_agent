Frequently Asked Questions

Who can become an LIC agent?
Anyone above 18 years of age with a minimum qualification of 10th standard (or equivalent) can apply to become an LIC agent. No prior experience is required, and we provide comprehensive training.
What is the process to become an LIC agent?
The process involves four steps: (1) Apply online or at an LIC branch, (2) Attend training, (3) Clear the IRDAI licensing exam, and (4) Start selling as an official LIC agent. Our team supports you at every step.
How much can an LIC agent earn?
Earnings are commission-based with unlimited potential. Agents earn commissions on policies sold, renewals, and bonuses for high performance. Top agents can earn significant incomes based on their efforts.
Is training provided for new agents?
Yes, LIC provides comprehensive training covering insurance fundamentals, sales techniques, and product knowledge. Training is free and designed to prepare you for the IRDAI exam and your career.
Can I work as an LIC agent part-time?
Yes, you can work as an LIC agent part-time. This flexibility allows you to balance other commitments while building your career in insurance.
What support does LIC provide to agents?
LIC offers extensive support, including training, marketing materials, access to 2,000+ branches, digital tools, and a network of 1.2 million agents for guidance and mentorship.
How do I take the IRDAI licensing exam?
After completing the mandatory training, you can register for the IRDAI exam through LIC. The exam tests basic insurance knowledge, and LIC provides study materials and mock tests to help you prepare.
